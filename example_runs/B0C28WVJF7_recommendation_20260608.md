### Edit 1 — Bullet 3: lead with concrete macros

**Before**

**After** *(numbers in brackets — fill in from the Supplement Facts panel before pasting)*

**Rationale (conversion):** The single biggest conversion gap in the comparison. Every direct competitor in the set gives hard numbers in their bullets — Skratch leads with "200 calories and 50g of complex carbohydrates per serving," Flow opens with "Maximize your power output with the research-optimal 0.8:1 Glucose-to-Fructose ratio." BPN's current bullet is entirely qualitative ("superior," "complete profile") and gives the shopper nothing to weigh against the alternatives one click away.

**Competitor reference:** Skratch Labs Super High-Carb (B08PH7232Y) — bullet 4: "Use 1 serving before exercise or as needed. 200 calories and 50g of complex carbohydrates per serving." Same Cluster Dextrin carb as BPN, so it's a true head-to-head; BPN currently loses on specificity.

### Edit 2 — Bullet 4: reframe the Informed Sport certification, drop the Manufacturing Date detail

**Before**
> THIRD-PARTY TESTED FOR BANNED SUBSTANCES: Informed Sport certified products are tested for prohibited substances ensuring you feel comfortable consuming. Date printed on bottle is the Manufacturing Date.

**After**
> COMPETE WITH CONFIDENCE — INFORMED SPORT CERTIFIED: Every batch is independently tested for over 250 substances banned in competition (WADA list), so collegiate, military, and pro athletes can fuel without worrying about a failed screen

**Rationale (conversion):** Informed Sport is one of the strongest credibility signals BPN has — it directly answers the "will this make me fail a drug test" question that endurance athletes (especially the collegiate, military, and pro segments) ask before buying. Today the bullet buries it in passive voice ("are tested for") and closes on a totally unrelated detail about the printed-bottle date, which distracts from the value prop at the bullet's last impression. Moving the manufacturing-date note out of the bullet and into the description (or a Q&A) lets the certification land cleanly. Calling out the user segments (collegiate, military, pro) lets the buyer self-identify.

**Competitor reference:** None of Tailwind (B01865XX8Q), Skratch (B08PH7232Y), or Flow Formulas (B0FNQFBS6S) mentions third-party banned-substance certification in their bullets. This is a defensible point of differentiation that BPN is currently underselling.

**Amazon rule citations:**
- *Amazon Pet Supplies Styleguide, p. 5* — bullets are "a snapshot of the product" customers "may finalise a purchase decision based on this information alone"; the current closing sentence undercuts that snapshot.
- *Amazon Pet Supplies Styleguide, p. 5* — "Reiterate important information from the title and description" and "be as specific as possible with product features and attributes."

**Rationale (conversion):** The current description is the listing's biggest dead zone — a shopper who scrolls down is high-intent, and right now they meet machine-generated text that doesn't even describe the product.
